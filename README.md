# Power_BI_Dashboards
This project presents a comprehensive Hotel Operations and Booking Performance Dashboard designed to analyze booking trends, customer behavior, and revenue efficiency. The dashboard enables hotel management to make data-driven decisions by identifying revenue leakage, operational gaps, and growth opportunities.

1) High Revenue but Underutilized Capacity :
Despite generating 2bn in revenue, the hotel operates at only 57.87% occupancy, indicating significant unused room inventory and opportunity for growth without increasing capacity.

2) High Cancellation Rate Causes Revenue Leakage :
With 24.83% of bookings cancelled, nearly 1 in 4 confirmed bookings fails to convert into actual stays, directly impacting revenue realization and operational planning.

3) Strong Pricing Does Not Guarantee Revenue :
The hotel maintains a healthy ADR of 12.7K, but RevPAR remains moderate at 7.35K, proving that high pricing alone does not maximize revenue without strong occupancy and realization.

4) Revenue Realisation is Only 70.15% :
Only 70% of expected revenue is actually realized, highlighting losses due to cancellations, discounts, and no-shows, and signaling the need for tighter booking and pricing controls.

5) Luxury Rooms Drive Maximum Revenue :
Presidential and luxury room classes generate the highest RevPAR, showing that high-end customers deliver stronger revenue efficiency and lower booking drop-offs.

6)  Mid-Tier Rooms Underperform :
Premium and Elite rooms show weaker booking success and lower RevPAR, suggesting a pricing or value perception gap that needs repositioning.

7) Booking Conversion is a Major Bottleneck :
Only 57.87% of booking attempts are successful, indicating friction in the booking journey related to pricing, availability, or payment processes.

8) Online Platforms Dominate but Increase Dependency :
Most bookings and customer ratings come from third-party online platforms, while direct offline bookings contribute the least, increasing commission costs and dependency risk.

9) Customer Stays Are Short-Term
The average length of stay is 2.37 days, indicating dominance of short or weekend stays, which limits customer lifetime value and total revenue per booking.
